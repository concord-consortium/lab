Student activities

1. Exploring the projection effect

In the following model, rays of light shine on a surface and heats it up. Start the simulation and then click on the applet window and type 'q' and 'w' to adjust the angle and observe how the heating effect depends on the angle.

Important Note: Sunlight is represented by a limited number of rays emitted into the box, each of which travels at a much slower speed than the speed of light. This is a computational approximation that does not reflect the nature of light energy.

  2. At what angle will the surface get the minimum heat?
