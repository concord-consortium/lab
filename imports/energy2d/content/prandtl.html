The Prandtl Number

Conductive vs. Convective Heat Transfer

Both conduction and convection happen in fluids. As heat transfer through both processes reduces the temperature difference, they can be considered as competing against each other in transferring heat. There are many different types of fluids, such as air, water, oil, or mercury. The rates of conduction and convection may be different in different fluids. Sometimes, conduction dominates. Other times, convection dominates. The Prandtl number is a parameter that can be used to roughly determine which process will "win":

Pr=ν/α

where ν is the kinematic viscosity and α is the thermal diffusivity.
